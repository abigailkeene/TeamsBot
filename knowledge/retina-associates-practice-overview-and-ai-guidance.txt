Purpose of This Document

This document is designed to be fed into an internal AI chatbot for all Retina Associates staff, including clinical, administrative, and support teams.

Its purpose is to help staff:
- Understand how Retina Associates operates
- Know which systems are used for which tasks
- Get guidance on common questions and workflows
- Know where to go for help and documentation

The AI trained on this document should act like a knowledgeable internal guide that understands the full practice, not just IT.


===============================
About Retina Associates
===============================

Retina Associates is a multi-location ophthalmology and retina specialty medical practice.

The practice focuses on diagnosing and treating retinal diseases and conditions using advanced imaging, in-office procedures, and coordinated care.

Everything the practice does centers on:
- Patient safety and outcomes
- Efficient clinic flow
- Accurate documentation and billing
- Secure handling of patient information


===============================
Departments at Retina Associates
===============================

Physicians

Physicians diagnose and treat retinal conditions, interpret imaging, perform procedures, and guide patient care plans.

They rely on accurate imaging, complete documentation, and smooth clinic operations.


Clinical Technicians and Imaging Staff

Technicians and imaging staff:
- Prepare patients
- Capture retinal images and diagnostics
- Support physicians during clinic
- Enter clinical data into systems

They depend on:
- Imaging devices and software
- Exam room computers
- Reliable logins and system performance


Front Desk and Scheduling

Front desk teams handle:
- Patient check-in and check-out
- Scheduling appointments
- Verifying demographics and insurance
- Managing patient flow

They rely heavily on:
- EHR and scheduling systems
- Phone systems
- Accurate patient records


Billing, Coding, and Prior Authorization

Administrative and billing teams manage:
- Insurance eligibility
- Prior authorizations
- Coding and claims submission
- Payment posting and follow-up

Their work depends on:
- EHR and Practice Management systems
- Payer portals
- Accurate clinical documentation


IT and Operations Support

IT supports all departments by:
- Troubleshooting issues
- Maintaining systems and devices
- Coordinating with vendors
- Building internal tools
- Improving workflows

IT decisions are made with clinic impact and patient care in mind.

Core Systems Used at Retina Associates
===============================

AdvancedMD (legacy system)

AthenaHealth / AthenaOne (current or transitioning system)

Used for:

Imaging and Diagnostic Systems

Productivity and Access

Microsoft 365 (Outlook, Teams, SharePoint)

Windows 10 and Windows 11 computers

Internal Tools and Resources

Custom tools built specifically for Retina Associates include:
- EyeHub, an internal knowledge base and documentation system
- EyeHubPA, a prior authorization tracking system
- Credentialing database
- Insurance and eligibility tools

These tools exist to reduce manual work and improve accuracy.


===============================
How Work Is Prioritized
===============================

Clinic-First Operations

Patient care always comes first.

System stability during clinic hours is critical.

Non-urgent updates are scheduled outside of patient-facing times.


System Changes and Downtime

Not all systems can be updated immediately.

Many devices require vendor coordination.

Testing is required before changes are made.


Documentation Culture

Processes are documented whenever possible.

EyeHub is the primary location for internal documentation.

Staff are encouraged to check documentation before escalating issues.


===============================
Data Security and Patient Privacy
===============================

Patient data is confidential.

Access is role-based.

Systems follow HIPAA-related requirements.

Staff should never:
- Share passwords
- Store patient data outside approved systems
- Install unauthorized software
- Change system settings without approval


===============================
New Staff and Intern Context
===============================

New staff and interns are expected to:
- Ask questions early
- Follow established procedures
- Use documentation
- Escalate issues when unsure

How the AI Assistant Should Help Staff
