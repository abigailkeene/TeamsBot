RETINA ASSOCIATES CORE VALUES AND EMPLOYMENT POLICIES

CORE VALUES

Accountability  
Taking responsibility for one's actions, decisions, and behaviors, and being answerable for the outcomes. Fulfilling obligations with integrity and transparency.

Patient-Focused  
Total dedication to patients, prioritizing compassion, respect, integrity, ethical practice, and excellence to ensure compassionate, safe, and ethical relationships.

Employee Development  
Enhancing employee skills, knowledge, and abilities through training, mentorship, and learning opportunities to support both personal growth and organizational goals.

Integrity  
Being honest, having strong moral principles, and consistently doing the right thing, even when no one is watching.

Respect  
Recognizing and valuing the worth of others and treating them with kindness, fairness, and consideration.

--------------------------------------------------

EMPLOYMENT-AT-WILL

Employment at RETINA ASSOCIATES is at-will, meaning either the employee or the company may terminate employment at any time, for any reason, with or without notice.

This handbook is not an employment contract. No representative is authorized to alter this policy.

--------------------------------------------------

EQUAL OPPORTUNITY AND INCLUSION

RETINA ASSOCIATES is an equal opportunity employer. All qualified applicants are considered without regard to race, color, religion, national origin, sex, pregnancy, age, marital status, sexual orientation, gender identity, disability, or military status.

--------------------------------------------------

AMERICANS WITH DISABILITIES

RETINA ASSOCIATES complies with all federal and state laws regarding employment of individuals with disabilities and provides reasonable accommodations when possible.

--------------------------------------------------

NEW HIRE PROCESS

Employment is contingent upon background check and drug testing. Terms are confirmed in writing and kept in personnel records.

--------------------------------------------------

WORK AUTHORIZATION

Employees must provide valid documentation proving legal eligibility to work in the United States.

--------------------------------------------------

EMPLOYMENT DOCUMENTATION

Any falsification or omission of employment information may result in termination.

--------------------------------------------------

INTRODUCTORY PERIOD AND EVALUATION

New employees undergo a 90-day introductory period with evaluations at 30, 60, and 90 days.

Annual performance evaluations occur each December.
