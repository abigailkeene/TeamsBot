1. Purpose
The purpose of screening patients before being seen by the doctor is to ensure that all necessary preliminary information is gathered to facilitate a smooth and efficient examination process. Screening helps with collecting vital information, performing baseline tests, identifying urgent issues, improving clinic efficiency, and enhancing patient care.

2. Scope
This SOP outlines the responsibilities and procedures for Clinical Technicians, including patient preparation, diagnostic testing, assistance during procedures, and equipment maintenance. It ensures standardized, high-quality support in clinical operations to enhance patient care and support the medical team in providing effective retina treatments.

3. Definitions
MDI: Electronic Health Record  
ABN: Advanced Beneficiary Notice  
VA: Visual Acuity  
IOP: Intraocular Pressure  

4. Responsibilities
Clinical Technician: One should be able to consistently perform all assigned screening duties within the recommended time frame and in accordance with clinic protocols within 6 months of employment.

5. Screening

5.1 Injections

1. Grab Superbill  
Retrieve the patient's superbill for the visit.

2. Select Patient Name  
In MDI, select the patient and move them into the screening room.  
Create a new encounter for today’s visit.

3. Prep Chart  
Prepare the patient’s chart with today’s diagnostic order or procedure details.

4. Call Patient  
Call the patient into the room.

5. Confirm Patient Information  
Verify patient’s name and date of birth.

6. Confirm Eye and Drug  
Confirm with the patient which eye will be treated and which drug will be used.

7. Review Changes  
Ask, “Any changes in your condition?”  
If no, proceed with obtaining consent.  
If yes, ask for details:  
“What changes?”  
“How long?”  
“Is this new or different from what you’ve experienced before?”

8. Consent  
Have the patient sign consent for the correct eye and drug.  
Obtain the ABN (Advanced Beneficiary Notice) if applicable.

9. Medical Evaluation  
Confirm at least one clinical element needed for evaluation.  
Check Visual Acuity (VA).

10. If VA Worsened  
If VA has worsened by more than two lines and does not improve with pinhole, dilate the affected eye.

11. Check Intraocular Pressure (IOP)  
Administer numbing drops.  
Measure and record the IOP in the chart.

12. Complete Charting  
Save and close the patient chart in the system.

Ideal Time Frame  
5–7 minutes is the target time to complete the screening process.  
Red Flag: If the process consistently takes more than 10 minutes, it should be addressed.

6. Review  
This will be reviewed annually and updated as necessary to ensure continued compliance and efficiency.
