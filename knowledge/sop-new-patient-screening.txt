1. Purpose
The purpose of screening patients before being seen by the doctor is to ensure that all necessary preliminary information is gathered to facilitate a smooth and efficient examination process. Screening helps with collecting vital information, performing baseline tests, identifying urgent issues, improving clinic efficiency, and enhancing patient care.

2. Scope
This SOP outlines the responsibilities and procedures for Clinical Technicians, including patient preparation, diagnostic testing, assistance during procedures, and equipment maintenance. It ensures standardized, high-quality support in clinical operations to enhance patient care and support the medical team in providing effective retina treatments.

3. Definitions
MDI: Electronic Health Record  
OCT: Optical Coherence Tomography  
FAF: Fundus Autofluorescence  
ABN: Advanced Beneficiary Notice  
VA: Visual Acuity  
IOP: Intraocular Pressure  

4. Responsibilities
Clinical Technician: One should be able to consistently perform all assigned screening duties within the recommended time frame and in accordance with clinic protocols within 6 months of employment.

5. Screening

5.1 New Patients

1. Grab Superbill:
Retrieve the superbill for today’s visit.

2. Select Patient:
Select the patient’s name in MDI and move them into the screening room.
Create a new encounter for today’s appointment.

3. Review Referral Letter (if available):
Glance over the referral letter.
Note most recent VA and reason for referral.

4. Prep Chart with Diagnostic Orders:
OCT for all patients.
Fundus photography for Drs. Rubino, Steuer, McFarland, and Kunjukunju.
FAF for Drs. Rubino and Kunjukunju.
Cube over the nerve for Dr. Rubino’s patients.

5. Call Patient:
Escort patient into screening room.

6. Confirm Patient Information:
Verify name and date of birth.

7. Chief Complaint:
Ask “What’s going on with your vision?”
Document: Patient states [summary].
Ask about flashes, floaters, and duration.
Ensure at least 4 elements.

8. Medical History Review:
Past medical history.
Past surgeries.
Drug allergies.
Family history.
Medication list.

9. Review of Systems:
Complete 2 bubbles.

10. Visual Exam:
Check VA.
Assess fields, pupils, motility.

11. Intraocular Pressure:
Administer numbing drops.
Check and document IOP.
If above 23, consult doctor before dilation.

12. Dilation:
Dilate both eyes unless instructed otherwise.

13. Complete Documentation:
Ensure chart is complete and accurate.

Ideal Time Frame:
15–25 minutes
Red flag if over 30 minutes.

6. Review
This SOP will be reviewed annually and updated as necessary.
