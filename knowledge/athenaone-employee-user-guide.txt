AthenaOne Employee User Guide
Retina Associates

Purpose of This Guide

This guide provides Retina Associates staff with step-by-step instructions for using AthenaOne for daily clinical and administrative workflows.

It is designed to help staff:
- Navigate AthenaOne confidently
- Complete common tasks efficiently
- Understand where information is located
- Follow consistent documentation and workflow standards

AthenaOne is the primary system used for scheduling, clinical documentation, billing, and patient management.


What Is AthenaOne?

AthenaOne (AthenaHealth) is Retina Associates’ practice management and clinical system used for:

- Scheduling appointments
- Managing patient demographics and insurance
- Clinical documentation
- Billing and claims
- Check-in and check-out workflows
- Provider charting


Logging Into AthenaOne

1. Open a supported web browser (Chrome or Edge recommended)
2. Go to the AthenaOne login page provided by IT
3. Enter your username and password
4. Complete multi-factor authentication if prompted

If you are unable to log in, contact IT support.


Patient Search

To locate a patient record:

1. Use the search bar at the top of the screen
2. Enter one of the following:
   - Patient name
   - Date of birth
   - Medical record number (MRN)
3. Select the correct patient from the results list

Always verify patient identity before proceeding.


Scheduling Appointments

1. Search for the patient
2. Open the patient chart
3. Select the Appointments or Scheduling section
4. Choose the provider, location, and visit type
5. Select an available appointment time
6. Confirm appointment details with the patient
7. Save the appointment

Ensure the correct visit type is selected to support proper billing and clinic flow.


Patient Check-In

1. Open the patient’s appointment
2. Verify patient demographics
3. Confirm insurance information
4. Collect copay if required
5. Mark the patient as checked in


Patient Check-Out

1. Open the patient encounter
2. Confirm provider documentation is complete
3. Schedule follow-up appointments if needed
4. Collect any remaining balances
5. Provide patient instructions
6. Complete checkout


Clinical Documentation (Providers & Technicians)

1. Open the patient chart
2. Navigate to the current encounter
3. Enter exam findings, imaging results, and notes
4. Review imported imaging and diagnostics
5. Sign and close the encounter

Documentation must be completed same day whenever possible.


Insurance & Eligibility

1. Open the patient chart
2. Navigate to Insurance section
3. Verify active coverage
4. Confirm eligibility status
5. Resolve any coverage issues before the visit


Billing & Claims

1. Ensure encounter documentation is complete
2. Confirm diagnosis and procedure codes
3. Submit charges
4. Monitor claim status
5. Follow up on denials


Messaging & Tasks

AthenaOne may be used for:
- Internal staff messaging
- Task routing
- Clinical follow-ups

Always follow internal communication policies.


Common Issues & Troubleshooting

If AthenaOne is slow or not loading:
- Check your internet connection
- Restart your browser
- Clear browser cache if instructed by IT

If you experience:
- Login issues
- Missing data
- System errors

Contact IT immediately.


Best Practices

- Never share your login credentials
- Always log out when leaving your workstation
- Verify patient identity before documenting
- Document encounters promptly
- Follow clinic workflow standards


When to Contact IT

Contact IT if:
- You cannot log in
- The system is not loading
- Data appears incorrect or missing
- You receive system errors
- You need access changes


Final Notes

AthenaOne is a mission-critical system for Retina Associates.
Accurate use supports patient safety, clinic efficiency, billing accuracy, and regulatory compliance.

Always follow documented workflows and escalate issues when unsure.
