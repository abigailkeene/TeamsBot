1. PURPOSE

The purpose of screening patients before being seen by the doctor is to ensure that all necessary preliminary information is gathered to facilitate a smooth and efficient examination process.

Screening helps with:
- Collecting vital information
- Performing baseline tests
- Identifying urgent issues
- Improving clinic efficiency
- Enhancing patient care

--------------------------------------------------
2. SCOPE

This SOP outlines the responsibilities and procedures for Clinical Technicians, including:

- Patient preparation
- Diagnostic testing
- Assistance during procedures
- Equipment maintenance

It ensures standardized, high-quality clinical operations to enhance patient care and support the medical team in providing effective retina treatments.

--------------------------------------------------
3. DEFINITIONS

MDI: Electronic Health Record  
ABN: Advanced Beneficiary Notice  
VA: Visual Acuity  
IOP: Intraocular Pressure  

--------------------------------------------------
4. RESPONSIBILITIES

Clinical Technician:
- Must consistently perform all assigned screening duties within the recommended time frame
- Must follow clinic protocols
- Expected to achieve competency within 6 months of employment

--------------------------------------------------
5. SCREENING PROCEDURE

--------------------------------------------------
5.1 FOLLOW-UP SCREENING WORKFLOW

1. Grab Superbill:
- Retrieve the patient’s superbill for today’s visit

2. Select Patient:
- Select the patient's name in MDI
- Move the patient into the screening room
- Create a new encounter for today’s appointment

3. Prep Chart:
- Review the chart
- Add any diagnostic orders or procedures for today’s visit
- This step should take no more than 1–2 minutes

4. Call Patient:
- Bring the patient into the room

5. Confirm Patient Information:
- Verify the patient’s name
- Verify the patient’s date of birth

6. Vision Check:
- Ask: “How’s your vision?”

7. Chief Complaint Documentation:
- Document: “Patient states [summary of issue].”
- Ask:
  - Any flashes or floaters?
  - Duration of the problem?
- Chief complaint must include at least 4 elements for completeness

8. Review Medical History:
- Ask: “Any changes to medications or medical history since your last visit?”
- If yes:
  - Update medication list
  - Update medical history as needed

9. Review of Systems:
- Under Review of Symptoms, check at least 2 bubbles

10. Visual Assessment:
- Check Visual Acuity (VA)
- Examine fields
- Examine pupils
- Examine motility

11. Intraocular Pressure (IOP):
- Confirm no allergies or intolerance to eye drops
- Administer numbing drops
- Measure IOP
- Record result in chart
- If IOP is above 23, ask the doctor if dilation is allowed

12. Dilation:
- Dilate both eyes unless otherwise specified in follow-up note

13. Complete Charting:
- Save and close the patient chart

--------------------------------------------------
IDEAL TIME FRAME

- Target completion time: 10–12 minutes
- Red Flag: If consistently taking more than 20 minutes, review workflow for improvement
