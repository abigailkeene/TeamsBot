PX Technology Employee User Guide

Purpose

This guide provides Retina Associates staff with instructions for using PX Technology for patient enrollment, prior authorization management, insurance coordination, and specialty medication workflow tracking.

PX Technology is used to:
- Enroll patients into specialty medication programs
- Manage prior authorizations
- Track insurance approvals and denials
- Monitor treatment status
- Maintain compliance documentation

PX Technology supports clinical operations, billing workflows, and patient access to treatment.

What Is PX Technology

PX Technology is a specialty medication workflow platform used to manage:
- Patient enrollment
- Prior authorizations
- Insurance benefit verification
- Treatment approvals
- Renewal tracking
- Expiration monitoring
- Clinical documentation

It is primarily used for injectable and specialty retina medications.

Who Uses PX Technology

PX Technology is used by:
- Prior authorization team
- Billing staff
- Clinical coordinators
- Specialty pharmacy coordinators
- Clinical leadership

Accessing PX Technology

1. Open a web browser
2. Navigate to the PX Technology portal
3. Sign in with your assigned credentials
4. Complete MFA if prompted

If login fails, contact IT support.

Patient Enrollment

PX Technology is used to enroll patients into specialty medication programs.

Enrollment includes:
- Patient demographics
- Insurance information
- Prescribing provider
- Diagnosis codes
- Medication details

Creating a New Patient Enrollment

1. Log into PX Technology
2. Select New Enrollment
3. Enter patient information
4. Enter insurance information
5. Select medication
6. Attach required documentation
7. Submit enrollment

Insurance Verification

PX Technology verifies:
- Insurance eligibility
- Pharmacy benefits
- Medical benefits
- Coverage requirements

Verification results show:
- Covered medications
- Prior authorization requirements
- Step therapy requirements
- Quantity limits

Prior Authorization Management

PX Technology tracks:
- Prior authorization submissions
- Approval status
- Denials
- Appeals
- Expiration dates

Submitting a Prior Authorization

1. Open patient record
2. Select Prior Authorization
3. Complete required fields
4. Attach clinical documentation
5. Submit to payer

Tracking Authorization Status

Authorization statuses include:
- Submitted
- Pending
- Approved
- Denied
- Expired
- Appeal in progress

Medication Tracking

PX Technology tracks:
- Medication start dates
- Renewal deadlines
- Authorization expiration
- Treatment schedules

This ensures patients do not miss doses.

Renewals

PX Technology sends alerts for:
- Expiring authorizations
- Pending renewals
- Missing documentation

Renewals must be submitted before expiration.

Documentation

PX Technology stores:
- Clinical notes
- Insurance forms
- Approval letters
- Denial letters
- Appeal documents

Documents must be complete and accurate.

Compliance

PX Technology maintains:
- Audit trails
- Submission history
- User activity logs

This supports payer audits and compliance requirements.

Common Issues & Troubleshooting

Cannot Log In
- Verify username and password
- Check MFA
- Contact IT if locked out

Missing Patient Record
- Verify enrollment was submitted
- Check search filters
- Confirm spelling and DOB

Authorization Pending Too Long
- Verify submission date
- Check payer requirements
- Follow up with insurance

Denied Authorization
- Review denial reason
- Gather required documentation
- Submit appeal

Expired Authorization
- Submit renewal immediately
- Notify clinical team

Security & Patient Privacy

PX Technology contains protected health information (PHI).

Staff must:
- Never share login credentials
- Never download patient data to personal devices
- Never email PHI outside approved systems
- Log out when leaving workstation

Support

For PX Technology issues:
- Document the issue
- Capture error messages
- Check patient record status
- Escalate to IT or billing leadership
