Microsoft Outlook Employee User Guide

Purpose

This guide provides Retina Associates staff with instructions for using Microsoft Outlook for email, calendar, and internal communication.

Outlook is the primary communication platform for Retina Associates and is used for:
- Internal communication between staff and departments
- Provider and clinic coordination
- Scheduling meetings and appointments
- Receiving system notifications and alerts

Logging Into Outlook

1. Open Outlook
   - On your PC, click the Outlook icon on your desktop or taskbar
   - Or go to https://outlook.office.com in your web browser

2. Sign in using your work email and password
   - Use your Retina Associates email address
   - Enter your Microsoft 365 password
   - Complete MFA if prompted

If you cannot log in:
- Make sure Caps Lock is off
- Verify you are using your full work email
- Try logging in from the web version
- Restart your computer
- Contact IT if login fails

Email Basics

Sending an Email
1. Click New Email
2. Enter the recipient in the To field
3. Add a subject line
4. Type your message
5. Click Send

Replying to an Email
- Click Reply to respond only to the sender
- Click Reply All to respond to everyone on the email
- Click Forward to send the email to another person

Attachments
1. Click Attach File
2. Select the file from your computer or OneDrive
3. Attach the file before sending

Inbox Management

Folders
- Inbox: All incoming emails
- Sent Items: Emails you have sent
- Deleted Items: Deleted emails

Creating Folders
1. Right-click Inbox
2. Select Create new folder
3. Name the folder

Using Search
- Use the search bar at the top of Outlook to find emails by sender, subject, or keyword

Common Outlook Problems & Solutions

Emails Not Sending

Possible causes:
- No internet connection
- Outlook is offline
- Mailbox is full
- Attachment is too large

Steps to fix:
1. Check your internet connection
2. Look at the bottom of Outlook — make sure it does not say “Working Offline”
3. Try sending the email again
4. Remove large attachments and resend
5. Restart Outlook
6. Restart your computer

Emails Not Receiving

Possible causes:
- Outlook is offline
- Connection issues
- Mailbox sync problem

Steps to fix:
1. Close and reopen Outlook
2. Check internet connection
3. Click Send/Receive
4. Restart your computer
5. Log into Outlook Web and verify if emails appear there

If emails appear in web but not desktop, contact IT.

Outlook Keeps Freezing or Crashing

Possible causes:
- Large mailbox
- Corrupt profile
- Add-in issues

Steps to fix:
1. Close Outlook
2. Restart your computer
3. Open Outlook again
4. If freezing continues, contact IT for profile repair

Search Not Working

Steps to fix:
1. Click inside the search bar
2. Press Enter after typing your search
3. Restart Outlook
4. Restart your computer

If search still does not work, contact IT.

Missing Emails

Possible causes:
- Accidentally deleted
- Moved to another folder
- Sent to Junk folder
- Archived automatically

Steps to locate:
1. Check Deleted Items
2. Check Junk Email folder
3. Use search bar
4. Check other folders

If still missing, contact IT.

Mailbox Full

Signs:
- Warning messages
- Unable to send emails

Steps to fix:
1. Delete unnecessary emails
2. Empty Deleted Items folder
3. Remove large attachments from Sent Items
4. Move old emails to folders

If mailbox is still full, contact IT.

Calendar

Viewing Your Calendar
1. Click the Calendar icon on the left side of Outlook
2. View your schedule by day, week, or month

Creating a Meeting
1. Click New Event
2. Add a title
3. Select date and time
4. Add attendees
5. Click Save

Accepting a Meeting Invite
- Click Accept to add it to your calendar
- Click Decline if you cannot attend

Meeting Not Showing on Calendar

Steps to fix:
1. Refresh Outlook
2. Restart Outlook
3. Check if you accepted the meeting
4. Check your calendar filters

Shared Mailboxes

Some departments use shared mailboxes.

To access a shared mailbox:
1. Click your profile icon
2. Select Open another mailbox
3. Enter the shared mailbox name

If you cannot access a shared mailbox:
- Contact IT to request access

Junk & Phishing Emails

Junk Email
- Junk emails are automatically filtered
- Check Junk folder regularly

Phishing Emails
- Do not click links
- Do not open attachments
- Do not reply

Report phishing to IT immediately.

Security & Best Practices

- Do not share your password
- Do not open suspicious links or attachments
- Report phishing emails
- Lock your computer when away from your desk
- Log out of shared computers

Support

If Outlook is not working:
- Restart your computer
- Try Outlook Web
- Check your internet connection
- Contact IT Support if the issue continues
