1. Purpose
The purpose of screening patients before being seen by the doctor is to ensure that all necessary preliminary information is gathered to facilitate a smooth and efficient examination process. Screening helps with collecting vital information, performing baseline tests, identifying urgent issues, improving clinic efficiency, and enhancing patient care.

2. Scope
This SOP outlines the responsibilities and procedures for Clinical Technicians, including patient preparation, diagnostic testing, assistance during procedures, and equipment maintenance. It ensures standardized, high-quality support in clinical operations to enhance patient care and support the medical team in providing effective retina treatments.

3. Definitions
• MDI: Electronic Health Record  
• Gtt: Eye Drops  
• QAM: Once in the AM  
• QID: Four Times a Day  
• QHS: Once at Night  
• OCT: Optical Coherence Tomography  
• VA: Visual Acuity  
• OU: Both Eyes  
• IOP: Intraocular Pressure  

4. Responsibilities
• Clinical Technician: One should be able to consistently perform all assigned screening duties within the recommended time frame and in accordance with clinic protocols within 6 months of employment.

5. Screening

5.1 Post-Ops

1. Prepare Materials  
Grab superbill and post-op bag for 1-day post-op patients.

2. Select Patient  
Select the patient’s name in MDI and move them into the screening room.  
Create a new encounter for today’s visit.

3. Prep Chart with Orders  
Review and prepare the chart with diagnostic orders and gtt information.  
1-day post-ops do not require OCTs (check the follow-up note; Dr. K may also omit OCTs for some 1-week post-ops).

4. Input Medication (gtts)  
Input prescribed eye drops for 1-day post-ops:  
- Atropine QAM  
- Prednisolone QID  
- Tobramycin QID  
- Ointment QHS  
Adjust if otherwise specified.

5. Call Patient  
Escort the patient into the room.

6. Confirm Patient Information  
Verify the patient's name and date of birth.

7. Post-Op Questions  
Ask: “How’s the post-op eye feeling?”  
Document response under Chief Complaint: “Patient states [summary of condition].”

8. Chief Complaint Documentation  
Only 2 elements are needed for post-op visits.  
Include relevant details from the Post-Op Element Tab (e.g., eye pain, no eye pain, using gtts as prescribed).

9. Review of Symptoms  
Complete 2 bubbles under Review of Symptoms.

10. Confirm Medication Usage  
For 1-day post-ops, review gtt instructions with the patient.  
For 1-week post-ops or more, confirm which drops the patient is using and how often.

11. Check Visual Acuity (VA)  
For 1-day post-ops, check VA of post-op eye only.  
For other post-ops, check VA OU.

12. Check Fields, Pupils, and Motility  
For 1-day and 1-month post-ops, perform checks on post-op eye only.  
For other post-ops, check OU.

13. Check Intraocular Pressure (IOP)  
Administer numbing drops and check IOP.  
For 1-day post-ops, check IOP of post-op eye only.  
For other post-ops, check IOP OU.  
If IOP is above 22, ask the doctor if dilation is okay.

14. Administer Eye Drops for 1-Day Post-Ops  
Administer post-op eye drops minus antibiotic ointment.  
Record administration time in the chart.

15. Dilate Eye  
Dilate the post-op eye only unless otherwise specified.

Important Notes  
Doctors prefer seasoned technicians for post-op patients.  
Follow procedure efficiently to ensure timely evaluations.

6. Review
Reviewed annually and updated as necessary.
