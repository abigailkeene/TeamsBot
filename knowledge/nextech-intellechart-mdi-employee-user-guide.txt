Nextech IntelleChart (MDI) Employee User Guide

Purpose

This guide provides Retina Associates clinical and administrative staff with instructions for using Nextech IntelleChart (MDI) as the primary Electronic Health Record (EHR) system for clinical documentation, patient charting, and encounter management.

MDI is used to:
- Document patient visits
- Manage clinical workflows
- Store medical records
- Track diagnoses and treatments
- Support billing and coding
- Integrate with imaging and diagnostic systems

MDI is a core clinical system and must be used accurately and consistently.

What Is Nextech IntelleChart (MDI)

Nextech IntelleChart is a specialty ophthalmology EHR system designed for retina practices.

It supports:
- Patient charting
- Exam documentation
- Imaging integration
- Treatment documentation
- Orders and prescriptions
- Clinical history
- Encounter tracking

MDI integrates with:
- Heidelberg Heyex
- Optos Advance
- AthenaHealth
- PX Technology
- RetinaOS

Who Uses MDI

MDI is used by:
- Physicians
- Clinical technicians
- Imaging staff
- Scribes
- Billing and coding teams
- Clinical coordinators

Accessing MDI

1. Open MDI from desktop shortcut or web portal
2. Log in using assigned credentials
3. Complete MFA if prompted

Access is role-based and permission controlled.

Patient Search

1. Enter patient name, DOB, or MRN
2. Select patient from results
3. Open patient chart

Patient Demographics

Patient demographics include:
- Name
- DOB
- Address
- Phone number
- Insurance
- Emergency contact

Verify demographics at every visit.

Encounter Workflow

1. Open patient chart
2. Select today’s appointment
3. Begin encounter
4. Document exam
5. Attach imaging
6. Enter diagnosis
7. Enter treatment plan
8. Sign encounter

Clinical Documentation

MDI supports:
- Structured templates
- Free-text notes
- Imaging attachments
- Historical comparisons

Documentation must be:
- Accurate
- Complete
- Signed by provider

Imaging Integration

MDI integrates with:
- Heidelberg Heyex
- Optos Advance
- Other imaging platforms

Images are linked directly to the patient chart.

Orders and Prescriptions

Providers can:
- Enter medication orders
- Submit prescriptions
- Document injections and procedures

Billing & Coding

MDI provides:
- Diagnosis codes
- Procedure codes
- Billing workflows
- Charge capture

Billing accuracy depends on proper documentation.

Chart Review

Staff can:
- Review prior visits
- Compare imaging
- Review treatment history
- Review diagnoses

Audit Trail

MDI tracks:
- Chart access
- Documentation edits
- Signature timestamps
- User activity

This supports compliance and auditing.

Common Issues & Troubleshooting

Cannot Log In
- Verify username and password
- Check MFA
- Contact IT if locked out

Slow Performance
- Check internet connection
- Restart workstation
- Contact IT

Missing Images
- Verify Heyex or Optos connection
- Confirm image transfer
- Contact imaging support

Encounter Won’t Close
- Check required fields
- Verify provider signature
- Complete documentation

Wrong Patient Chart
- Exit immediately
- Notify supervisor
- Document correction

Security & Patient Privacy

MDI contains protected health information (PHI).

Staff must:
- Never share login credentials
- Never leave charts open unattended
- Never download patient records
- Log out when leaving workstation

Support

For MDI issues:
- Document the issue
- Capture error messages
- Contact IT or clinical systems support
